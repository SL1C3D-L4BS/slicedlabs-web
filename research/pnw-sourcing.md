# PNW local sourcing — Spokane / Inland NW (2026)

> Real, verifiable organizations only. Items marked **verify** need a direct
> call/email before you commit (operating status, wholesale terms, current rates).

## Food hubs & distributors (start here)
- **LINC Foods** — Spokane farmer/worker-owned co-op food hub (est. 2015).
  Sources from 60+ small organic/regenerative farms; wholesale arm **LINC Market**
  supplies restaurants/grocers; aggregates 75+ growers into one order.
  *3012 N Nevada St, Spokane.* → the single best first call for local sourcing.
- **Northwest Food Hub Network** — farmer-owned co-op network (200+ producers, WA+MT);
  LINC is the Spokane member. Cross-regional reach.
- **Second Harvest Inland Northwest** — hunger-relief network; the Farm-to-Food-Pantry
  channel for end-of-day surplus (a donation program, not a supplier). **verify** terms.

## Farmers markets (2026 season)
- **Spokane Farmers' Market** — Sat + Wed AM, 2nd & Chestnut (Browne's Addition).
- **Spokane Valley Farmers Market** — Fri 4–7pm, Jun–Aug, Scale House Market (The Quarry).
- **Emerson-Garfield** — Fri 3–7pm, Jun–Sep, Emerson Park.
- **Hillyard** — Thu 3–7pm, Jun–Oct, NE Community Center.
- **Perry Street Thursday Market** — Thu 3–7pm, May–Oct, South Perry.
- **Kendall Yards Night Market** — Wed 5–8:30pm — heavy prepared-food/food-truck
  venue → **truck cross-promotion + first-service target.**

## Farms / ranches / producers
**Produce:** Casa Cano Farms (Valleyford — organic veg + pork/beef) · **Green Bluff
Growers** (30+ farms ~15min N — cherries, peaches, apples, pumpkins; Walters', Siemers,
Hidden Acres, Beck's Harvest House, High Country).
**Beef/pork/poultry:** Holbert Angus (Cheney, grass-fed) · Lazy R Ranch (regenerative
bulk) · Four E Farms (red Angus) · Ramstead Ranch (grass-fed beef/lamb/pork/poultry) ·
Twin Calves Ranch (Cheney — **verify** status).
**Dairy/cheese:** Columbia Community Creamery (Chewelah — cheese since 2026) · Clover
Mountain Dairy (organic A2 Jersey) · Schoonover Farms (into Yoke's/Huckleberry's).
**Grain/flour:** **Shepherd's Grain** (grower-owned, regenerative, traceable) ·
Palouse Heritage (heritage wheat/barley).

## Breweries & cideries (collab targets)
~21+ Spokane breweries ("Ale Trail"). Best values-fit:
- **The Grain Shed** — worker-owned co-op, South Perry, uses local grain → strongest
  farm-to-table alignment.
- **No-Li Brewhouse** — flagship; markets "Spokane Style" w/ Inland NW ingredients.
- **Bellwether**, **Whistle Punk**, **Brick West**, **Iron Goat** (pizza+brewery model).
- **Cideries:** Liberty Ciderworks, One Tree (100% WA apples), Twilight (Green Bluff).

## Inland NW seasonality (±2 wk; plan the chaos menu around it)
| Month | In season |
|---|---|
| Jan–Mar | Storage: apples, potatoes, onions, squash, cabbage, root veg |
| Apr | Asparagus begins, greens, rhubarb, early peas |
| May | Asparagus peak, radishes, peas, late-May strawberries |
| Jun | Strawberries peak, cherries begin, greens, peas |
| Jul | Cherries peak, apricots, peaches begin, berries, squash, early tomatoes, corn |
| Aug | Peaches/nectarines + tomatoes peak, corn, peppers, melons, plums |
| Sep | Apples begin, pears, peppers, squash, pumpkins, late corn |
| Oct | Apples peak, pears, pumpkins, root veg, hardy greens |
| Nov–Dec | Storage apples/pears, squash, root veg, hardy greens |
Year-round: pasture meats, dairy, grain, eggs, honey.

## Networks & "local" proof
- **Eat Local First Collaborative** — directory + WA Food & Farm Finder; LINC anchors Spokane.
- **WSDA Farm to School / Farm to Food Pantry** — institutional + surplus channels.
- No single binding "farm-to-table cert" — proof rides on LINC's roster, USDA organic,
  Eat Local First listing, or WSDA GAP.
