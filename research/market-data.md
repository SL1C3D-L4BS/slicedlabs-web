# Market data — the case, with sources (2026)

> Every number cites a real source. Confidence + caveats are honest on purpose —
> that's the brand. Use the "load-bearing six" publicly; keep the caveats in mind.

## The load-bearing six (safe to publish)
1. **US food truck market ≈ $2.8B, 13.2% CAGR 2020–2025** (IBISWorld).
2. **Food trucks net ~6–9% (up to 15% owner-run) vs ~1–3% for restaurants** (Toast / Harvest).
3. **#1 NRA "What's Hot 2025" trend = Local Sourcing & Sustainability; 64% of consumers
   call local important** (National Restaurant Association).
4. **YouTube Food & Beverage = 4th-largest category; +32% YoY views Q1 2025; 99%
   creator-driven** (Pixability via NetInfluencer).
5. **Creator economy ≈ $190–250B, ~22% CAGR** (Grand View Research et al.).
6. **Email beats social: ~8% vs ~3% conversion, 5–10× engagement, owned channel**
   (ClickMinded / DMA / EmailToolTester).

## Industry detail
- 92,257 food trucks in the US in 2025, +16.9% YoY (IBISWorld). Market plateaued
  in 2025 (−0.2% YoY) → **the gold rush is over; operator quality wins now.**
- Avg annual revenue ~$250K–$500K (mean ~$346K, Toast). Startup $100K–$250K
  (used $30–60K; new build $80–125K).
- Global food-truck market $5.42B (2024) → ~$7.87B by 2030, 6.3% CAGR (Grand View).

## Spokane / WA
- City of Spokane ≈ 231K (2026); metro ≈ 470K, +1.08% YoY (Census / MacroTrends).
- 2025 James Beard semifinalist: Anna Vogel, Italia Trattoria (Spokane) — real
  national-level talent in the scene.
- Cork District: 15 walkable downtown wineries; Kendall Yards Night Market = active
  street-food venue.
- **Honest caveat:** no verifiable 2025–26 "top US food city" ranking. Say "a
  growing scene with Beard-level talent," not "one of America's best food cities."

## Local / farm-to-table demand
- Sustainability + local sourcing = NRA's #1 2025 trend; 64% of consumers value local.
- WTP premium for local ≈ 34% (academic meta-analysis) — **but field experiments show
  stated ≫ revealed WTP.** Treat 34% as a ceiling. PwC 2025: ~9.7% premium for
  sustainable products.
- Millennials + Gen Z skew hardest toward local/low-waste (NRA).

## Media / creator / owned-audience
- Food & Beverage on YouTube: 457B views Q1 2025 (+32% YoY); 6.74M channels; 99% of
  top channels are creator-driven, 1% brand (Pixability). → individuals beat corporates.
- Email ROI commonly cited ~$36/$1 (DMA-era figure, repeated — quote it as such).
  beehiiv avg open 37.67% across 15.68B sends.
- "Build in public" lacks a clean headline stat — the support is indirect (74% of
  marketers say content generates demand, HubSpot). Lead with the *proof* story,
  not a fake number.

## Do NOT publish
- "Spokane = top food city" past 2019.
- "$36 per $1 email" without naming it a DMA-era figure.
- "Build-in-public grows audiences X%" (no source).
- "34% more for local" without the stated-vs-revealed caveat.
