The first item we ever cooked on camera. Cheap commodity beef, a screaming cast-iron, and a sauce named after the brand. This is the chaos menu in one plate: buy the commodity, build the edge.

## You'll need

**The smash (per burger)**

- 3 oz (85 g) 80/20 ground chuck, loosely balled — *don't* pack it
- 1 soft potato bun, butter-toasted
- 1 slice American cheese (yes, American — it melts like nothing else)
- Thin-sliced white onion
- Kosher salt, coarse black pepper

**Spectrum sauce** (the four-ingredient edge)

- 4 tbsp mayo
- 1 tbsp ketchup
- 1 tbsp yellow mustard
- 1 tsp dill pickle brine + a pinch of smoked paprika

## Method

1. **Sauce first.** Whisk everything for the spectrum sauce and rest it in the fridge while you cook — 10 minutes is enough for the paprika to bloom.
2. **Heat the iron.** Cast-iron or flat-top on the highest heat you've got, a full 5 minutes. It should be *uncomfortable* to hold your hand over.
3. **Smash.** Drop a loose beef ball, lay parchment on top, and smash hard and flat with a stiff spatula for 10 seconds. Thinner = more crust. Salt and pepper the raw side.
4. **The crust is the point.** Don't touch it for 90 seconds. When the edges go lacy and brown, scrape (don't lift) under the crust, flip, and immediately add cheese and a few onions.
5. **30 seconds, then build.** Let the cheese melt, then stack onto the toasted bun bottom. Sauce the top bun generously.
6. **Eat standing up.** It's a truck burger. That's the format.
