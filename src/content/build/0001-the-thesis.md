Episode one is always the same: a guy with a brand and a thesis. Every episode after is a rung climbed in public — the truck, the first lot, the first product — with the real numbers shown.

Here's the thesis. The "own your slice / build in public" space is drowning in people selling the dream and never showing the machine. So we do the opposite: we show the machine. The real ~$85k truck budget. The real permit list — L&I before the health permit, the commissary you have to return to daily, the gray-water tank 15% larger than the fresh. The real unit economics. The real failures.

That's not a content strategy. It's the moat. Almost nobody shows the fundamentals, because most of them don't have any. Radical transparency about how the thing actually works *is* the deep value.

**We don't pitch. We prove.**

The plan is boring on purpose: truck → first lot → property + an owned commissary → the homestead → a skills center. We don't start the next thing until the current one throws off cash. We buy every commodity tool and build only our edge — the content and the audience nobody can rent us.

Watch me build mine from the ground up. Then go build yours.

Own your slice.
