Most food brands hide the recipe. It's the secret sauce, the thing you protect. We do the opposite: we publish ours in public — the inspiration, the technique, the source, and the full build, step by step.

There are about 29 recipes so far, split across four sections. The Anchors are the dishes the menu stands on. Seasonal Drops are exactly what they sound like — they show up when the ingredient does and leave when it goes. The Dough Lab is where the bread work lives. And Sweet is the finish.

Every one of them is Pacific Northwest sourced and tied to the season. That's not a slogan we paste on top — it's the constraint we cook inside. If it isn't good and isn't local and isn't in season, it doesn't make the cut.

A few real ones, so this isn't abstract. The Inland Cubano — the Cuban classic, built with what's actually here. The Palouse Falafel, named for the wheat country it comes out of. The Forager Melt, which leans on what the season turns up. The Mother Dough milk bread, out of the Dough Lab, built on a starter we keep alive.

**Cook the season; show the source.**

Here's the move that makes the whole thing work twice. The recipes are the food menu and the blog at the same time. The same page that tells you how to make the Forager Melt is the page that tells you why it's on the truck this month and where the ingredients came from. One artifact, two jobs. We don't write marketing about the food and then separately make the food — the recipe is the marketing, because the recipe is the proof.

And every drop has a reason. Nothing lands on the menu because it photographs well. It lands because the season handed it to us, because a local grower had something worth building around, because the technique was worth teaching. When the season turns, the menu turns with it, and we show you why on the way through.

Honest caveat: 29 recipes is a start, not a library. The Anchors will hold; the Drops will rotate hard and some will be one-offs that never come back. That's the point of a seasonal menu — it's supposed to move.

So that's the system. Cook what the season gives us, show exactly where it came from, and let the recipe carry both the meal and the message.

The future, sliced.

Own your slice.
