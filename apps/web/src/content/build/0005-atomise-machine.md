The build isn't a thing we do and then make videos about. The build is the documentary. We film it as it happens, and that footage is the raw material for everything downstream.

The system is simple to say and hard to do: film once, publish six. One shoot gets atomised into six platform-native cuts — not the same clip reposted six times, but six pieces shaped for where each one lands. YouTube is the media spine; everything else hangs off it.

Why lead with food on YouTube? Because the numbers point there. Food is the fourth-biggest category on the platform, and roughly 99 percent of the top food channels are individual creators, not brands. Read that again. The big food channels are people, not logos. That's the opening for a documented build — a real operator showing real work walks into a lane the brands can't fake their way into.

**Brands can't fake a documented build. That's the whole opening.**

But attention on a platform isn't the asset. The platform owns that, and the algorithm gives and takes it on a whim. So we treat every video as a funnel toward the one thing we actually own: the list.

Owned email converts several times better than rented social — and we own it outright. No algorithm deciding who sees us. No de-platforming risk. No middleman renting us back our own audience. The newsletter is the asset. The videos are how we fill it.

That's the bottom rung of the offer ladder, and it's the one we never skip: own the list. Social is rented reach we use to find people. Email is the relationship we keep. Every cut we publish, every recipe drop, every episode of the build points back to the same ask — get on the list, where the algorithm can't get between us.

Honest caveat: six cuts from one shoot is the goal, not a guarantee. Some shoots only carry three. Some weeks the spine is a single long video and nothing else. The discipline is the point — film with the six in mind, capture enough to cut wide — even when the output comes up short.

So the engine is: live the build, film it as a documentary, atomise one shoot into six, point all of it at a list we own. The work makes the content, the content fills the list, and the list is the thing nobody can rent us.

The future, sliced.

Own your slice.
