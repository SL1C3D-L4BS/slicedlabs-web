## The headline
A solid Spokane food-truck launch lands around **$85,000** for the build + permits +
launch. Add a working-capital buffer and the all-in number is closer to **~$93k** —
that's the difference between "the truck exists" and "the truck survives month one."

Industry context (IBISWorld / Toast): food-truck startups run **$100k–$250k** at the
high end; a used truck is **$30–60k**, a new custom build **$80–125k**. The $85k path
below uses a quality **mid-build** and disciplined choices.

## Why these numbers work
- **Margins:** food trucks net **~6–15%** owner-run vs **~3–5%** for restaurants
  (industry benchmarks). Lower entry, the truck moves to the crowd.
- **The catering lever:** events fill the gap between services — higher ticket,
  booked ahead. The booking funnel is already live at slicedlabs.com/truck.
