The first thing we ever cooked on camera, and still the one people ask for. It's a Spokane burger with a Havana accent: smashed local beef, the bright garlic-citrus **mojo** of a Cuban sandwich, and sweet onions cooked down in a local IPA. Buy the commodity (cheap beef), build the edge (the sauce and the onions). That's the whole menu in one plate.

## You'll need

**Mojo aioli** (the Cubano move — make this first)

- 4 tbsp mayo
- Zest + 1 tbsp juice of an orange
- 1 tbsp lime juice
- 1 small garlic clove, grated
- ½ tsp ground cumin, ½ tsp dried oregano
- Salt to taste

**IPA sweet onions** (the PNW move)

- 1 large sweet onion (Walla Walla if you can get it), thinly sliced
- 1 tbsp butter
- A splash of a local IPA or amber (about ¼ cup)
- Pinch of salt

**The smash (per burger)**

- 3 oz (85 g) 80/20 local ground chuck, loosely balled — *don't* pack it
- 1 slice aged Swiss (Gruyère is the gourmet swap)
- A few thin dill pickle rounds + a thin swipe of yellow mustard
- 1 soft potato or brioche bun, butter-toasted
- Kosher salt, coarse black pepper

## Method

1. **Mojo aioli.** Whisk it all together and rest it in the fridge. Ten minutes lets the garlic and citrus bloom; an hour is better.
2. **Onions, low and slow.** Butter in a pan on medium-low, onions + a pinch of salt. When they go soft and gold (~15 min), deglaze with the IPA and cook it down until syrupy. Set aside.
3. **Heat the iron.** Cast-iron or flat-top on the highest heat you've got, a full 5 minutes — it should be *uncomfortable* to hold your hand over.
4. **Smash.** Drop a loose beef ball, lay parchment on top, smash hard and flat for 10 seconds. Thinner = more crust. Salt and pepper the raw side.
5. **Crust, then cheese.** Don't touch it for 90 seconds. When the edges go lacy and brown, scrape (don't lift) under the crust, flip, and lay on the Swiss to melt.
6. **Press it like a Cubano.** Build: mojo aioli on both buns, the patty, pickles, mustard, a spoon of IPA onions. Close it and press 20 seconds with a second pan so the bun crackles.
7. **Eat standing up.** It's a truck burger. That's the format.

**PNW notes:** add a thin griddled ham slice to take it full Cubano, or stripe a little huckleberry preserve on the top bun — Spokane in one bite. Use whatever local beer's in the fridge for the onions.
