We're a food company. That means we have a front-row seat to how much good food gets thrown away — at the household level, at the truck, across the whole chain. **FreshSave is the arm we're building to do something about it.**

To be honest about where this is: FreshSave is a **commitment, not a finished nonprofit.** The sequence is the truck first — it has to throw off cash before we stand up a mission arm with real funding behind it. We're writing this page now so you can hold us to it later, and watch it get built in public like everything else.

## What it will do

- **Teach the skill, not the guilt.** Most "reduce your waste" advice is nagging. We'd rather show the cook: how to use the whole vegetable, how to read a fridge, how to turn the odds-and-ends into a meal worth eating.
- **Publish our own numbers.** The truck will track its own waste and we'll show the numbers — the same receipts-first honesty we hold everything else to.
- **Amplify, don't compete.** Spokane and the Inland NW already have food banks, gleaning networks, and food hubs doing the hard work. FreshSave's job is to send people and attention their way, not to plant a flag on top of them.

## How you can be part of it

The free [food tools](/kitchen/tools) are the first concrete thing — a waste log anyone can use, today, for free. Beyond that, get on the list and you'll see every step of this arm getting built.
