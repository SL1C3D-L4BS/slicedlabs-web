The reason FreshSave exists is in the numbers. Globally, roughly **a third of all food produced is never eaten.** In the U.S., a big share of that loss happens at the very end of the chain — in homes and kitchens — which is exactly where a few small habits change the outcome.

For a household, that waste is real money: produce that wilts, leftovers that get forgotten, the loaf that goes stale. It adds up to hundreds of dollars a year for a typical family.

## Our commitment, in receipts

We're not going to quote impact we haven't earned. Here's what we'll actually publish as the truck runs:

- **Our own waste, weighed.** What the truck throws away each service, and what we did to shrink it.
- **What the [food tools](/kitchen/tools) catch.** In aggregate and anonymous — how much logged waste turns into "huh, I should use that first."
- **Where the surplus goes.** Any usable surplus routed to local partners instead of the bin.

That's the deal: we show the math, including the parts that aren't flattering. [Follow the build](/build) or [grab the tools](/kitchen/tools).
