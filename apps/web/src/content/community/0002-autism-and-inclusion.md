Food work has always been a path to good jobs for people the rest of the economy overlooks — including neurodivergent folks who thrive in the structure and craft of a kitchen. As SlicedLabs grows, **we're committing part of what we make to autism support and to building an inclusive kitchen.**

We want to be precise about the stage this is at, because the [honesty law](/about) applies here most of all:

- This is a **standing commitment**, funded as the truck and the rest of the business throw off cash — not a claim that the checks have already gone out.
- When we sponsor an org or an event, we'll **name it and show the amount** here. No "proud supporter" badges without the receipt.
- On the hiring side, the goal is a kitchen and a workflow that work for different minds — clear systems, sensory-aware stations, and real mentorship.

If you run an autism or inclusion organization in the Inland NW and there's a fit, reach out — we'd rather build the relationship early than announce one late.
