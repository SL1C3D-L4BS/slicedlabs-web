A food truck lives or dies on its community. We don't want to just sell to Spokane — we want to be **useful** to it. That's not a slogan; it's the only model that survives.

Here's how we plan to show up, sequenced honestly against what the business can actually carry:

- **Buy local, loudly.** Source from Inland NW farms and ranches and name them on camera — the sourcing story is the marketing, and it keeps money in the region.
- **Show the work at the markets.** Riverfront, Kendall Yards, South Perry — we'll be where the neighborhood already gathers, and we'll cook in the open.
- **Teach what we know.** As the [workshops](/workshops) come online, a share of seats go to people who'd never pay full freight — students, career-changers, anyone trying to build something with food.

We'll add the named partners and the receipts here as they become real. No vanity logos, no "proud to support" without the proof behind it.
